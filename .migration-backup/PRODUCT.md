# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Helicopter operators, owners, brokers, and engineers, worldwide. They arrive in a
high-stakes, time-pressured frame: they need Part 145 maintenance, Part 147 type
training, a pre-purchase inspection, continued-airworthiness support, or urgent AOG
help, and they are deciding whether this independent UK organisation is credible and
fast enough to trust with their aircraft. Often referred or arriving from search,
frequently on mobile, occasionally mid-crisis (aircraft on ground).

## Product Purpose

The marketing site for THEHELIGROUP, the only stand-alone combined UK CAA Part 145
maintenance + Part 147 training organisation in the UK, independent of any operator or
AOC. The site exists to establish credibility quickly, communicate the breadth of
services and the approved aircraft types, and convert serious enquiries into a
conversation, primarily through the embedded AI chat agent that qualifies the visitor
and either books a call or routes AOG situations straight to a duty engineer. Success is
a qualified operator or engineer trusting the brand enough to start that conversation.

## Brand Personality

Warm, brief, professional, British. Confident because the team is genuinely good, not
because it is performing. Dry warmth over corporate polish ("spot on", "no drama", never
"kindly" or "world-class"). Three words: precise, independent, unflappable. The emotional
goal: an operator should feel they have found serious, senior people who will be straight
with them and move fast.

## Anti-references

- Generic aviation-corporate: stock skies, "your trusted partner in the skies" platitudes.
- SaaS-template landing pages: gradient hero metrics, identical icon-card grids, a tiny
  uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section.
- Loud salesmanship: exclamation marks, buzzwords (streamline / empower / world-class /
  cutting-edge / next-generation).
- Overstated capability. The brand's honesty about what it can and cannot do is part of
  the trust; the design must never oversell.

## Design Principles

1. Credibility over decoration. Every element should make a senior aviation professional
   trust them more.
2. Be straight. Show real capability (types, approvals, the team) plainly; honesty is the
   differentiator, so mirror it visually.
3. Earn the conversation. The chat agent is the conversion engine; the page builds enough
   trust that starting it feels natural, never pushed.
4. Precision is the brand. Craft, alignment, and restraint signal the same care they bring
   to an airframe.
5. Move fast, calmly. Rapid mobilisation is the promise; the site should feel responsive
   and unflustered, motion included.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA in both themes: body text >=4.5:1 against its background in light and dark.
Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every scroll and cinematic effect (the
bold-motion direction makes this non-negotiable). Keyboard-operable header nav, dropdown,
chat widget, and theme toggle. Honour `prefers-color-scheme` for the initial theme, with a
manual toggle that persists across visits.
